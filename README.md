# Data-Science-Project
This repository is for my SoftUni project, for the Data Science module.
This readme file's purpose is to help plan the project, as well as document my original idea and any changes that occur to it. Changes will be marked with **(date of change)**

Working project title: Impact of High‑Profile Space Events on US produced Sci‑Fi Movies (1960s–2026)

Idea: 
I want to see whether there is a connection between major space events, the movies Hollywood makes and audience's interest in them. For the purposes of this project, 
a space event is defined as a major space‑related commercial milestones like those during the Cold War space race, the launch of the international space station, the recent Artemis II
NASA mission and others. The connections I will be examining for:

1. Do space events increase the number of Sci-Fi movies, that Hollywood makes? - Because movie production is dependent on many different factors, I will search for a connection within
2-3 years after the event.
   
2. Do space events increase audience's interest in Sci-Fi movies? - Here, I will examine the generated revenue and registered audience (commercial success) of Sci-Fi films, adhering
to point 1's timeframe and films.

3. Do space events influences new Sci-Fi films' plots? Do elements of recent space events make it into new Sci-Fi movies? - Keyword search, across the movies' summaries, for terms
derived from or associated with the specific space event, which was closest to the movies release date. (the dataset I have for this is incomplete, so whether point 3 makes it into
the project is still uncertain).

So, H0 is "Space events have an affect on the movies being made and audience's interest"/ H1 is "Space events do not have an affect on the movies being made and audience".

Style and Structure:
The writing style of this project will be that of a popula science article. The structure will be:

**Abstract**
**Introduction** - brief overview of - 1. major space events; 2. cultural importance of Sci-Fi cinema. Some discussion on how they have been tied, leading to the research questions
and hypotheses.
**Literature Review** - comment on whether the literature is rich or poor on the matter and present what I'm able to find.
**Data and Methodology** - present and describe the datasets; present and describe the variables; present and describe my approach; comment on existing limitations.
**Results and Discussion** - visualize the data and comment on it; break it down into 3 parts (Idea section).
**Conclusion** - summarize what I have done and the key findings; comment on what they mean and how the work can be expanded further in the future.
